Tracking gas station visits

BY CELIA LUDWINSKI / W209 SECTION 3 / SPRING 2018 / MIDS, UC BERKELEY

Gas station visits

Each point on the following graph depics a gas purchase at a gas station for a particular Toyota Corolla since the car's purchase. The overall trend shows approximately a fairly consistent usage of the car for driving distance (see the generally linear relationship) with some interesting deviations e.g. more driving through 2013 and a dip in driving distance from 2014 into the beginning of 2015.

Odometer readings at the time of each gas station visit

Data source

When her car was purchased in April 2012, Celia implemented a system for tracking the gas station visits. Every visit was documented in a hand-written notebook in the glovebox with four data points: date, odometer reading, current gas cost in usd per gallons, and the number of gallons purchased. This data was digitally transcribed on Jan 26, 2018.
